Data Sources

The analysis of airline delays at Seattle-Tacoma International Airport leverages three primary data sources, each offering unique insights into different aspects of air travel and aircraft operations.

Flight Departures Statistics from the Department of Transportation

This dataset offers from the Department of Transportation's extensive statistics on flight departures. It encompasses a wide array of fields critical for analyzing delays, including:

  • Carrier Code: Identifier for the airline operating the flight.
  • Date (MM/DD/YYYY): The date of the flight.
  • Flight Number: The specific flight number.
  • Tail Number: The aircraft's unique registration number.
  • Destination Airport: The intended destination airport code.
  • Scheduled Elapsed Time (Minutes): The planned duration of the flight.
  • Delay Carrier (Minutes): Delays attributed to the carrier.
  • Delay Weather (Minutes): Delays caused by weather conditions.
  • Delay National Aviation System (Minutes): Delays related to the national aviation system's operations.
  • Delay Security (Minutes): Delays due to security issues.
  • Delay Late Aircraft Arrival (Minutes): Delays resulting from the late arrival of the aircraft.

Releasable Aircraft Database from the FAA

Available directly from the FAA's official website, this comprehensive database includes several key files that detail aircraft registration and specifications:

  • Aircraft Registration Master File: Lists all registered aircraft.
  • Aircraft Dealer Applicant File: Information on aircraft dealer applications.
  • Aircraft Document Index File: Index of documents related to aircraft.
  • Aircraft Reference File by Make/Model/Series Sequence: Detailed make and model information.
  • Deregistered Aircraft File: Records of deregistered aircraft.
  • Engine Reference File: Specifications and information on aircraft engines.
  • Reserve N-Number File: List of reserved N-Numbers for aircraft.

The Global Airport Database (GADB)

The GADB is a freely available database encompassing over 9,300 airports worldwide, from major international hubs to smaller regional airports. It provides comprehensive details such as:

  • IATA Code: The International Air Transport Association code.
  • Name: The official name of the airport.
  • Country: The country where the airport is located.
  • City: The city nearest to the airport.
  • Latitude-Longitude Position: Geographical coordinates of the airport.

This SQL query is instrumental in correlating flight details from Alaska Airlines' departures at Sea-Tac from 2015 to 2023 with specific aircraft registration data, ensuring the aircraft's registration was valid at the time of the flight and that the flight occurred after the aircraft's certification. By linking to processed aircraft model details, we can gain insights into the aircraft models in use, facilitating a deeper analysis of potential factors influencing carrier delays.

        SELECT
        aa.*,
        ar.`Name`,
        ar.`Year mfr`,
        ar.`CertIssueDate`,
        ar.`LastActivityDate`,
        ar.`Expiration Date`,
        pa.*
    FROM (
        SELECT *,
            SUBSTRING(`Tail Number`, 2) AS `Tail_Number_Extract` -- 'Tail Number' starts with an N, so this gets rid of that
        FROM alaska_air_seatac_departure_2015_to_2023
    ) AS aa
    LEFT JOIN aircraft_registration_2015 ar
    ON aa.`Tail_Number_Extract` = ar.`N_Number`
    AND DATE(ar.`Expiration Date`) > STR_TO_DATE(aa.`Date` (MM/DD/YYYY), '%m/%d/%Y') -- Aircraft Registration Does Not Expire before the flight date
    AND STR_TO_DATE(aa.`Date` (MM/DD/YYYY), '%m/%d/%Y') > DATE(ar.`CertIssueDate`) -- Flight Date is after the Aircraft has been Certified
    JOIN `processed_aircraft_registration_model_details_2015` pa
    ON ar.AircraftMFRModelCode = pa.`MFR_MDL_CODE`
