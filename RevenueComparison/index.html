Introduction

Despite only 4.96% of vendors (artists/labels) on the platform selling merchandise, this revenue stream has proven to be incredibly lucrative. In fact, in just the 24 days studied, merchandise sales accounted for approximately 11% of Bandcamp's revenue, amounting to over a million dollars.

While there are many different forms of merchandise sold, T-Shirts are the most popular.

There were approximately 16,127 shirts sold, generating $383,788.68 in revenue.

This figure does not account for bundle deals, where musical releases are solid with physical merchandise. For instance, a t-shirt may be sold with a vinyl record. 315 bundle deals included a t-shirt, generating $34,391.92 in revenue.

Although there are at least 1943 vendors (artists/labels) who sold t-shirts on the platform, individual vendor's total revenue varies greatly.


After analyzing all the vendors (used here to refer to both artists and record labels), I have categorized them into three distinct groups:

Elite Vendors:

  • This group includes the renowned French Techno Label SAIKE and the popular Australian Rock Band King Gizzard & The Lizard Wizard.

Premium Vendors:

  • This cluster consists of around a dozen vendors, including artists like Boy Harsher, Chromeo, and Avalon Emerson, as well as labels like RuptureLDN. Each vendor individually generated approximately at least $1,600 in revenue. The median t-shirt price among these vendors is $26.

Standard Vendors:

  • These are the rest of the vendors on the site who are selling t-shirts.

Vendors, on average, will receive approximately only $365* in t-shirt revenue a year.

With so little revenue potential relative to the total initial costs to print merchandise coupled with the risk of excess inventory and long timelines to move this inventory, for many vendors, making merchandise is often not worth the risk. However, the sum is a whole of its parts, and this is where an opportunity lies...

* This number was calculated by taking the median revenue ($28) for vendors who sold t-shirts during our time frame and extrapolating that to the entire year. Seasonality was not factored in.

By either purchasing an enterprise solution or acquiring a "Print-on-Demand" Company, Bandcamp will be able to leverage its large vendor base of artists & record labels to offer competitive merchandise rates relative to wholesale providers.

This is achieved by guaranteeing a high volume of production for a merchandise supplier. There are three potential impacts to revenue that this opportunity presents.

1. Taking a % of the Reduced Merchandising Production Costs From the Enterprise Solution Implementation as Revenue

For example, if Bandcamp negotiated a 20% enterprise discount, a shirt that previously cost a vendor $10 upfront product would now cost the vendor $9 with Bandcamp taking $1 from the transaction. If vendors choose to handle merchandise sold through Bandcamp outside of Bandcamp's primary merchandise producer, they still have the option to do so.

Changing different parameters such as Bandcamp's enterprise discount rate, shirts sold, and the average price per shirt produces different outcomes and effects different revenue streams. A general approximation is that 584,000 t-shirts are sold on the platform in a year.

2. Increased Sales of Existing or Sold Out Products

For example, a vendor may have an existing product that is sold out but wishes to sell more of it but is concerned about just how many of that new item they may sell. "Print-to-Order" Merchandising would allow them to do so. If a vendor chooses to set a limit on the number of a certain item to create a "limited release", they still have the option to do so.

3. Encourages Vendors to Create New Products due to Less Risk + No Upfront Costs

The reduced risk associated encourages artists and record labels to experiment with merchandise. Bandcamp possesses a wealth of data and can use an artist's fanbase and musical tags to recommend additional merchandise lines based on what similar artists are selling. In addition to the potential revenue streams, creating an integrated system provides additional incentives for vendors to move all their merchandising operations to Bandcamp.

What are some additional Vendor benefits?

1. No Risk or Upfront Costs

Unlike traditional wholesale merchandising, there are no upfront costs involved. Additionally, if vendors choose to bulk order, they can receive even greater discounts. If vendors feel confident, they can move a minimum number of units, they can choose to order them and handle the merchandise shipping themselves, as currently done on Bandcamp. It is among vendors who have the confidence to take on these order volumes upfront that the cost per unit becomes much more competitive with traditional wholesale purchasing.

2. Reduced Administrative Costs and Time Spent

There is no requirement for a place to store merchandise or to worry about inventory management. Time-wise, this model frees up artists and labels from spending the time physically packaging and mailing items.

3. Providing Tailored Business Recommandations

This will take time to fully develop but I can see their being major benefits for smaller and up-and-coming artists. For example, an artist may be uncertain about how much merchandise to produce for an upcoming tour, but with the help of Bandcamp's recommendations, they can better estimate the demand and avoid under- or over-production. Even if the artist sells out of merchandise during the tour, fans can still purchase discounted items that will be shipped directly to their homes. Moreover, the platform has the potential to recommend optimal touring routes and destinations based on travel costs and fan geographic data. Overall, the platform's business recommendations could help level the playing field for smaller artists and labels, and make it easier for them to navigate the complex world of music business.
