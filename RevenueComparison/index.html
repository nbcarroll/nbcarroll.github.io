What Data is Used in this Analysis?

One Million Transactions on the platform from September 9th, 2020 to October 2nd, 2020.

How Has This Data Been Processed?

To generate more detailed categories of data from the dataset, Python was utilized. For instance, a transaction that involved a tote bag was previously coded as simply 'p', which denoted a physical item, without any additional categorization to specify that the item belonged to the 'Tote Bags' sub-category under the broader 'Accessories' category. However, by analyzing the 'item_description' feature, more granular information about each transaction was generated. In addition, additional features were created such as 'Buyer/Seller Country Match' to enable further analysis in the future.

Bandcamp has evolved into a comprehensive marketplace for not only music, but also merchandise.

Despite the fact that only 4.96% of vendors (artists/labels) on the platform sell merchandise, this revenue stream has proven to be incredibly lucrative. In fact, in just the 24 days studied, merchandise sales accounted for approximately 11% of Bandcamp's revenue, amounting to over a million dollars.

While There Are Many Different Merchandise categories, T-Shirts are the most popular.

There were approximately 16,127 shirts sold, generating $383,788.68 in revenue.
This figure does not account for bundle deals, where musical releases are solid with physical merchandise. For instance, a t-shirt may be sold with a vinyl record. 315 bundle deals included a t-shirt, generating $34,391.92 in revenue. **Although there are at least 1943 vendors (artists/labels) who sold t-shirts on the platform, individual vendor's total revenue varies greatly.** Among those who sold t-shirts, two generated more revenue than anyone else: the French Techno Label **SAIKE** + the Australian Rock Band **King Gizzard & The Lizard Wizard**. After these two outliers were removed, there is an other group of artists that called High Revenue Artists/Labels which produced some interesting insights.
