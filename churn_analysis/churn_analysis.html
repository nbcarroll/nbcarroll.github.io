Introduction

Objective

  • To predict customer churn in the telecommunications industry. This involves identifying patterns and factors that contribute to customers discontinuing their service. Understanding these elements is crucial for developing strategies to enhance customer retention.

Background

  • The telecommunications industry is a dynamic and rapidly evolving sector, characterized by intense competition and technological advancements.
  • In this highly competitive environment, the churn rate, which represents the proportion of customers who discontinue their services within a given period, emerges as a critical metric. A high churn rate not only affects the company’s revenue but also indicates potential issues in customer satisfaction and service quality.
  • This project draws inspiration from the concept that understanding and predicting customer behavior is key to retaining them. By analyzing comprehensive customer data, companies can develop targeted customer retention programs. The adage “Predict behavior to retain customers,” as highlighted by IBM Sample Data Sets, encapsulates the essence of this endeavor.

Key Takeaways & Recommendations

  1. Fiber Optic Internet: This is the greater predictor of Customer Churn. It is worth exploring Fiber Optic Internet more in depth to assess why customers appear to be churning the most.
  1. Digital Payment Experience: Since electronic checks have the second highest churn, improving the user experience or offering incentives to use other payments such as Automatic Credit Card Payments could be beneficial.
  1. Contract Flexibility: The high churn rate for month-to-month contracts suggests that perhaps offering incentives for longer-term commitments could be beneficial. Alternatively, identifying and addressing the reasons for dissatisfaction among these customers could be key.
  1. Targeted Surveys and Feedback: For the areas identified with high churn, targeted customer surveys could provide more insights into the reasons for churn.
  1. Customer Retention Programs: Programs targeted at the first month or the first payment could potentially have a large impact, as these are the areas where the churn rate is high.

Data Collection and Preparation

Dataset Description

Each row in the dataset corresponds to an individual customer, with columns detailing various attributes. The dataset encapsulates a diverse range of information, structured into several key categories:

  1. Churn: Central to the dataset is the 'Churn' column, which identifies customers who have discontinued their services within the last month. This binary attribute is crucial for understanding customer retention and turnover.
  1. Services Subscribed: The dataset details the array of services each customer has subscribed to. This includes basic phone services, multiple lines options, internet services, and a variety of digital offerings like online security, online backup, device protection, technical support, and options for streaming TV and movies.
  1. Account Information: It provides insights into the customer's account history and preferences. This includes the tenure of the customer (how long they've been with the company), the type of contract they are on, their chosen payment method, whether they use paperless billing, and financial aspects such as monthly charges and total charges accrued.
  1. Demographic Information: The dataset also covers demographic aspects of the customers, including their gender, age range, and family status, such as whether they have partners or dependents.

Standard Scaling

  • When you're working with a dataset that includes both one-hot encoded categorical features and numerical features, using a StandardScaler can be a bit nuanced.
  • If your dataset contains both types of features and you decide to use StandardScaler, you should apply it only to the numerical features.
  • You can achieve this by scaling the numerical features separately and then combining them back with the one-hot encoded features before training your model. Here's a basic approach to doing this with Python and pandas:

Here's how you can do it:Key Points:- Split Data First: Splitting the dataset before applying SMOTE prevents the synthetic samples from leaking into the testing set, ensuring that the test data remains untouched and is a true representation of real-world data.- Apply SMOTE to Training Data Only: SMOTE is applied only to the training data (X_train, y_train). This step balances the training data, helping the model learn better from the minority class.- Train and Evaluate on Different Sets: The model is trained on the resampled training set and then evaluated on the original test set. This helps to assess how well the model generalizes to unseen data.

Model Evaluation

Different metrics were used to assess the models…

Recall

Recall (also known as sensitivity or true positive rate) measures the proportion of actual positives that were correctly identified by the model. High recall is important in churn analysis if:

The cost of missing a customer who is about to churn (a false negative) is high. For instance, if losing a customer means significant revenue loss and if the company has effective retention strategies that can be deployed if a churn risk is identified.

The goal is to capture as many at-risk customers as possible, perhaps because the business has a high churn rate that needs to be addressed urgently.

Precision

Precision measures the accuracy of the positive predictions made by the model. In other words, it's the ratio of true positive predictions to the total number of positive predictions (both true positives and false positives).

In churn analysis, if a model predicts that certain customers will churn (positive prediction), precision tells you what proportion of these predicted churners did indeed churn.

A high precision indicates that when the model predicts churn, it is usually correct, while a low precision suggests that many customers predicted to churn did not actually churn.

F1-Score

F1-score is the harmonic mean of precision and recall. It is a single metric that combines both precision and recall to give a balanced measure of a model's accuracy in classifying data.

In churn analysis, the F1-score tells you how well the model balances the accuracy of its churn predictions (precision) with its ability to detect as many actual churn cases as possible (recall).

Support

Support refers to the number of actual occurrences of each class in the dataset. In a classification report, it indicates the number of samples for each class (such as 'churned' and 'retained') that were present in the test set.

  • In churn analysis:
    • The support for the 'churned' class tells you how many customers in your test dataset actually churned.
    • The support for the 'retained' class indicates how many customers did not churn.

Insights and Interpretation

Feature Importance

  • The most significant features influencing churn appear to be 'TotalCharges', 'tenure', and 'MonthlyCharges', which fall under the 'Account Info' category.
    • This suggests that the amount customers spend and the length of their relationship with the company are decisive factors in their decision to stay or leave.
  • Features associated with 'Services Subscribed', such as 'OnlineSecurity_Yes' and 'InternetService_Fiber optic', also play a substantial role, indicating that the type and term of services subscribed to are critical in retention strategies.
  • Across both Random Forest and Logistic Regression models, Fiber Optic Internet service is found to be a significant factor in if customers will churn. This internet service deserves further investigation to see what customers complaints are.
  • 'Demographic' features like 'gender' and 'SeniorCitizen' have less influence on churn, implying that personal attributes are less predictive of churn compared to billing and service-related factors.

Conclusion and Recommendations

Summary of Findings

Model Performance Comparison: Through the comprehensive analysis and comparison of various predictive models, this investigation revealed that the Random Forest with Undersampling emerged as the most effective model in predicting customer churn. This model demonstrated a superior balance between precision, recall, and F1-score, indicating its robustness in identifying churn cases while maintaining a high rate of correct predictions. The Random Forest with Undersampling model consistently showed strong performance across these metrics, particularly in terms of recall, which is crucial in minimizing the risk of overlooking potential churners.

Significance of Undersampling: The application of undersampling in preparing the dataset for the Random Forest model was pivotal in achieving these results. This approach effectively addressed the class imbalance issue inherent in our dataset, allowing the model to better learn the characteristics of the churn cases.

Insights into Customer Churn: The project also provided valuable insights into the factors contributing to customer churn.
